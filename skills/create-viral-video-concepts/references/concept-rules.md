# Concept Rules

## Required Difference

A new concept must differ from recent posts in at least four of these six dimensions:

- Main subject
- Setting
- Primary action
- Surprise or escalation
- Visual treatment
- Camera language

## Short-Video Structure

- Frame 1: immediately readable visual hook
- Seconds 1-5: one escalating action
- Seconds 5-7: surprising payoff
- Second 8: loopable return or match cut
- No on-screen text; captions are added after rendering
- Compose for a centered 9:16 frame

## Avoid

- Repeating "cat dances in a room" with cosmetic substitutions
- Generic montage prompts
- Multiple unrelated scenes
- Dialogue-dependent jokes
- Logos, public figures, copyrighted characters, or unsafe stunts
- Concepts requiring accurate text generation

## Good Variation Examples

- A tuxedo cat conducts a ghost orchestra in an abandoned subway; every baton swing relights the station.
- A tiny cat mechanic repairs a moon rover during a meteor shower; the final spark becomes the opening star.
- A claymation cat chef fights a runaway noodle tornado in a neon food truck.
- A noir detective cat follows glowing pawprints through a rain-soaked miniature city.
